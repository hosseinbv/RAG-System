## Q: What does Fusion 360 do?

Fusion 360 is an all-in-one cloud-based software that integrates 3D CAD, CAM, CAE, and PCB design, enabling users to go from design to manufacturing faster [3]. It supports direct, surface, parametric, mesh, or freeform modeling, provides detailed visualizations with exploded views and animations, and allows design and engineering for aesthetics, form, fit, and function [3]. The software includes tools for generative design, topology and shape optimization using cloud-based machine learning and AI, and supports simulation to test designs under real-world conditions [3]. It also offers comprehensive electronics and PCB engineering tools, including SPICE simulation and support for 16-layer PCB systems [3]. Fusion 360 enables collaboration through integrated cloud tools, manages product data, and supports additive manufacturing (including FFF and PBF for metal 3D printing), CNC milling (2 to 5-axis), and manufacturing-aware design automation [3]. It is used by global teams to ideate, create, collaborate, and manage product development processes efficiently [3].

## Q: What is Autodesk Maya used for?

Autodesk Maya is used for 3D animation, visual effects, modeling, simulation, and rendering, specifically for film, TV, and games. It provides a robust toolset for shaping, grooming, and bringing characters from concept to reality, including tools for animating attributes, joints, bones, and IK handles. Maya supports polygon and NURBS modeling, sculpting, and interactive grooming for realistic hair and fur. It also includes powerful simulation tools like Bifrost for physically accurate simulations and features such as the Time Editor, Cached Playback, and shape authoring for lifelike animation. Additionally, Maya supports integrated Arnold rendering, GPU and CPU rendering, look development with Hypershade, and procedural effects via MASH. The software is also enhanced with AI capabilities that allow natural language text prompts to control scenes, automate tasks, and improve creative workflows [1][2][4][5].
